Types of Attacks

Passive Attacks

Image credits to techdifferences.com

  • Attackers eavesdrop or monitor data transmission

    The 2 sub-categories of passive attacks

  1. Release of message contents
    • The sender wants to send a private message or email to receiver.
    • Passive attacker(interceptor) reads sender's message but does not modify the original message.
    • By using encryption, a message could be masked in order to prevent the extraction of the information from the message, even if the message is captured.
  2. Traffic Analysis
    • The attacker sniff the network; and
    • Attempt to analyze encoded messages to come up with likely patterns
    • e.g. by using a protocol analyser.

Active Attacks

Image credits to techdifferences.com

  • Active attacks involves modification to the contents of the original message.
  • Emphasizes on the detection of the attack and recovery from any disruption or delay caused by it.

    The 3 sub-categories of active attacks

  1. Interruption: affects availability of information systems
  2. Modification: affects the integrity of a message
  3. Fabrication: affects the authenticity of communication
Modification
  • Replay attack: the attacker captures and resends a message
    • For example, an attacker can gain access to a resource by resending authentication information to confuse the destination host
  • Alteration: change original message
    • The attacker captures and modifies the content of original message

Differences between Active and Passive Attacks

Active Attack Passive Attack
Any modifications made to data? Yes No
Any harm done to the system? Yes No, but potentially in the future
Threat done? Integrity and Availability Confidentiality
Emphasises on? Detection Prevention
